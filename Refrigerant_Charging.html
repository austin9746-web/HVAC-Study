❄️ Charging Walkthrough

Step-by-step subcooling & superheat method with live diagnosis

Subcooling Method (TXV Systems)

Used for systems with a TXV (thermostatic expansion valve). Measure liquid line pressure and temperature to determine charge level.
Connect gauges to service ports. Run system 10-15 minutes to stabilize.
Read liquid (high side) pressure from your gauges.
Look up the saturation temperature for that pressure on the PT chart.
Measure actual liquid line temperature with a clamp thermometer near the condenser outlet.
Calculate: Subcooling = Saturation Temp − Actual Liquid Temp
Compare to target (typically 10-15°F). Adjust charge as needed.

Superheat Method (Fixed Orifice Systems)

Used for systems with a fixed metering device (piston, capillary tube). Measure suction line pressure and temperature.
Connect gauges. Run system 10-15 minutes to stabilize.
Read suction (low side) pressure from gauges.
Look up saturation temperature for that pressure on PT chart.
Measure actual suction line temp with clamp thermometer 6" from compressor.
Calculate: Superheat = Actual Suction Temp − Saturation Temp
Compare to target. For fixed orifice, also check outdoor temp charging chart from manufacturer.

When to Use Which Method

Subcooling Method

Use when: TXV (thermostatic expansion valve) is the metering device. The TXV automatically adjusts superheat, so you use subcooling to set the charge. Target: 10-15°F subcooling (check manufacturer specs).

Superheat Method

Use when: Fixed orifice (piston or cap tube) is the metering device. With no TXV to regulate, superheat directly indicates charge level. Target: 8-12°F (varies by conditions — use manufacturer charging chart).

Key Rule: Low subcooling or high superheat = undercharged (add refrigerant). High subcooling or low superheat = overcharged (recover refrigerant). Always weigh charge in when possible — it's the most accurate method.
A2L Systems (R-454B): Same charging methods apply, but use A2L-rated recovery equipment, leak detectors, and follow charge limits. Do NOT mix with R-410A.
