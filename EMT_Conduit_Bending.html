💡 EMT Conduit Bending

Hand Bender Calculator & Reference

Stub-Up (90° Bend)

A 90° bend to make a stub that rises straight up. Subtract the take-up (deduct) from your desired stub height to find where to place the bender arrow.
How to bend: Make your mark, place the bender ARROW on the mark, foot on bender, pull handle to 90° using the spirit level on the bender.

Back-to-Back 90° Bends

Two 90° bends creating a U-shape. After making the first stub, flip the conduit and use the STAR/NOTCH mark on the bender to line up the second bend.
Procedure: 1) Make first stub. 2) Measure desired distance between bends from the back of the first bend. 3) Mark that point. 4) Flip conduit in bender, align STAR/NOTCH mark on the bender with your mark. 5) Bend to 90°.

Offset Bend

Two equal-angle bends to move conduit around an obstacle. Enter the depth of offset and your bend angle to get the distance between bend marks.
Procedure: 1) Calculate travel (distance between marks). 2) Make two marks on conduit at that distance apart. 3) Place first mark at arrow, bend to angle. 4) Flip conduit 180°, place second mark at arrow, bend to same angle. Keep bends in the same plane (anti-dog).

3-Point Saddle Bend

Goes over an obstacle without cutting the conduit. Three bends: a center bend at the full angle, two outer bends at half the angle, all in opposite directions.
Procedure: 1) Mark center of obstruction on conduit. 2) Mark outer bend distances from center. 3) Bend center mark first (full angle). 4) Flip and bend each outer mark (half angle). Compensate for shrink!

4-Point Saddle Bend

For larger obstructions. Four bends create a flat-topped saddle. Uses two offset bends — one going up and one coming back down.
Procedure: Essentially two offset bends. Four marks total. Bend 1&2 are the first offset (up), bends 3&4 are the second offset (back down). Account for shrink on both offsets.

Kick / Single Bend

A single bend at any angle. Used to change direction of a conduit run at a specific point.
